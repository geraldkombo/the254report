# MAJI Sentinel — One‑Page Funding Summary

## Problem
Kenya’s 47 counties face persistent, uneven gaps in safe drinking water access, sanitation coverage, water quality compliance, and utility performance. Decision-makers often rely on annual PDFs and fragmented dashboards, which slows response to drought, contamination, service outages, and sanitation-related disease risks.

## Solution
MAJI Sentinel is a production-ready, open-source water & sanitation intelligence platform that consolidates authoritative public datasets into county-level, nightly-updated scores and alerts. It provides a desktop-friendly map dashboard, a documented REST API, and automatic Emergency PDF briefs when thresholds are breached.

## What MAJI Sentinel Delivers
- County choropleths and operational layers: water points, service risk signals, and alert hotspots.
- Nightly intelligence scores (0–100): water access, sanitation, water quality, utility performance, governance readiness, climate resilience, and a composite index.
- Watch/Warning/Emergency alerts tied to transparent rules (e.g., high open defecation, high NRW, low lpcd, outbreak signals), with Emergency auto-generated PDF reports.
- Exportable evidence: CSVs for county scores and water points, plus API endpoints that support integration into partner workflows.
- Web3-ready accountability: signed “oracle” endpoints that can be consumed by smart contracts for pay-for-performance verification (optional; no high-risk identity dependency).

## Why It’s Grant‑Worthy
- Public value: turns open data into accountable, repeatable decision infrastructure for SDG 6 delivery.
- Rapid adoption: one-command deployment via Docker Compose on any modern desktop OS.
- Open-source by design: supports local ownership, adaptation, and ecosystem contributions.
- Multi-stakeholder fit: relevant to regulators (WASREB/WRA), counties, utilities, CSOs, donors, and researchers.

## Use of Funds (Typical Grant Workplan)
- Data expansion: production connectors for PDFs (WASREB), DHIS2 aggregates (HisKenya), WRA GIS layers, NDMA bulletins, and WPDx.
- Validation & QA: county-by-county data quality workflows, indicator coverage scoring, and audit trails.
- Advanced analytics: short-horizon risk prediction for water supply gaps and sanitation outbreak conditions.
- Partnerships: co-design with counties/utilities and capacity building for sustained adoption.

## Measurable Outcomes
- Reduced time-to-insight from weeks/months to daily refresh cycles.
- Faster detection of high-risk counties and indicators (water quality, sanitation, NRW, drought stress).
- Auditable reporting for donor programmes, with consistent metrics and reproducible computation.
