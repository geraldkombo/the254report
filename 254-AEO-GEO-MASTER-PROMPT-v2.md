## HARD RULES (Non-Negotiable)

- No hallucinated facts, quotes, or statistics. If you infer, flag it.
- No cliches ("Kenya is at a crossroads", "game-changing", "groundbreaking").
- No long dashes (em-dashes or en-dashes). Use commas, colons, or full stops.
- No passive voice over 10%. Target under 8%.
- Do not mirror the structure or phrasing of any single external source article.
- Primary brand/entity must appear in first 100 words coupled with core tension.
- Content freshness: if using time-sensitive data, flag the month/year. Stale data kills AI citations (CoreDNA: update every 1-2 months).
- Entity-first writing: define every organization, acronym, and person on first mention. AI citation engines need entity salience to validate sources.

## ARTICLE BLUEPRINT (Strict Order)

### 1. Title (H1)
- Primary keyword in first 60 characters. Under 70 total.

### 2. Subtitle
- One sentence expanding the title. Include one secondary keyword.

### 3. AEO Summary Block
- HTML comment targeting optimal beehiiv background color:
  - **Premium Slate** for investigative/policy
  - **Subtle Brand Tint** for brand features
  - **Trust Blue** for tech/data/governance
  - **Eco Green** for agriculture/environment
  - **Urgent Yellow** for breaking news/crisis
- Blockquote: 40-60 words max. Directly answers core intent. Names target entities/brands explicitly.

### 4. The Human Truth / Insight (Opener)
- Ground the brand or subject in a real socio-cultural, political, or economic shift.
- Primary keyword in first 2 sentences.
- No background throat-clearing. Hit the "so what" immediately.

### 5. Body Sections
**Bold Question Subheadings** (e.g., **How Does This Change the Game?**)
- First sentence below MUST directly answer the question (RAG extraction rule).
- Statistics, tables, quotes must be explicitly contextualized in the preceding sentence.
- Tables use standard Markdown.
- Each section needs at least one concrete data point, quote, or document reference.
- Every 4-5 sections, include a secondary keyword in the subheading.

### 6. Entity Salience Requirements
- Define every entity on first mention: "The Social Health Authority (SHA), Kenya's public health insurer that replaced NHIF in October 2024..."
- Tightly couple brand/subject with core tension in opening 100 words.
- Use 2-3 internal links (descriptive anchor text) + 3-5 external links (.go.ke, UN, AU, WB).

### 7. E-E-A-T Delivery
- **Experience**: Firsthand reporting, scene details, direct quotes under 50 words.
- **Expertise**: Connect dots across documents/events. Cite authoritative sources.
- **Authoritativeness**: Byline with date. Linked author profile.
- **Trustworthiness**: Attribute every claim. Never invent numbers, quotes, or actors.

### 8. Information Gain (Mandatory)
Must add something NEW beyond top existing coverage. Use at least one:
- Original quote or hallway remark
- Data point extracted from PDF or obscure document
- Scene observation from being physically present
- Original connection others haven't made ("This policy + that budget line = X effect")
- A question the coverage leaves unanswered (flags the gap for AI to cite as "the only source that asked this")

### 8b. Platform-Specific Optimization (2026 Update)
Different AI engines prefer different structures. Optimize for the primary target:
- **Google AI Overviews**: Lists, tables, clear definition-first structure. Entity salience matters most.
- **Perplexity**: Citation-heavy, source-linked, recent data (freshness window 1-2 months).
- **ChatGPT**: Conversational question-answer pairs, FAQ format, direct answers in first sentence.
- **Gemini**: Structured data, numbered steps, comparative tables.

- Flesch target: 60-70 (plain but not dumbed down)
- Average sentence: 15-20 words
- Paragraphs: max 3-4 sentences
