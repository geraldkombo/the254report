## Motivation / Background (500 words max)

### Title: Three Betrayals, Seven Decades: How an Unpublished Manuscript, Paul Muite's Landmark Case, and a 2025 Custody Death Expose Kenya's Unbroken Pattern of State Impunity

**What the investigation reveals:**

This investigation is the first to connect three distinct events across seven decades using sources that have never been combined: the handwritten manuscript of former detainee Eliud Mutonyi Wanjie (Kenya National Archives), the testimony of Hon. Paul Muite at the Wakati Wetu Festival 2025 documenting the 2013 London Mau Mau case, and the 2025 killing of blogger Albert Omondi Ojwang at Central Police Station, Nairobi.

The investigation makes four original contributions:

First, it uses Muite's framework of three betrayals (colonial land theft, colonial detention violence, post-independence suppression) to explain why the custody death pattern has persisted across fundamentally different political systems.

Second, it draws on Wanjie's previously unexamined manuscript to document the Kapenguria trial defence logistics, the camp-by-camp architecture of the colonial detention network (Nairobi West, Athi River, Mackinnon Road, Manyani, Sayusi Island, Hola), and the movement's internal justice system. These details are absent from standard historical accounts.

Third, it reveals that President Jomo Kenyatta's government re-gazetted Mau Mau as a terrorist organization in 1968, a classification that remained until 2003 and legally prevented survivors from filing compensation claims for forty years.

Fourth, it positions the June 2025 death of Albert Ojwang not as an isolated incident but as the current iteration of a pattern that has survived colonialism, independence, and the 2010 constitution.

**Investigative techniques:**

Four months of archival research at the Kenya National Archives accessing Wanjie's uncatalogued manuscript. Cross-referencing against colonial office records, Hansard parliamentary debates, and secondary historical accounts. Six months of court monitoring from June to December 2025 tracking the Albert Ojwang murder case, IPOA investigation updates, police press statements, and government pathologist disclosures. Direct reporting from the Wakati Wetu Festival on October 22, 2025, where Paul Muite gave his three-betrayals testimony. Protester interviews on Kimathi Street during the June 2025 demonstrations.

**Impact:**

The investigation provides the first unified evidentiary framework connecting colonial custody deaths, the post-independence suppression of Mau Mau survivors, the 2013 compensation program's failures, and modern police custody killings. It challenges three narratives simultaneously: the colonial nostalgia narrative that Kenya has progressed beyond such practices, the exceptionalism narrative that modern deaths are isolated failures, and the compensation narrative that the 2013 settlement resolved colonial justice claims. It provides an evidence base for IPOA reform and re-examination of the 2013 compensation program's verification process.

**Challenges:**

Wanjie's manuscript is not digitally indexed at the Kenya National Archives and was discovered through a detainee-name cross-reference. Police withheld CCTV footage and delayed post-mortem results in the Ojwang case. Muite's testimony at a cultural festival (not a courtroom or parliamentary setting) required verification against the 2013 settlement terms and British parliamentary records.

## Notes from Previous Winners

- **Dewald van Rensburg (2025 winner, amaBhungane)**: Won for exposé into Johannesburg's multibillion-rand illegal gold trade, implicating a pastor and a fugitive. Judging emphasized: forensic financial skills, persistence over 20-year career, public interest impact.
- **Mustapha K. Darboe (2025 runner-up, Gambia)**: Won for investigation into sale of former dictator Yahya Jammeh's assets, which led to parliamentary inquiry and officials being indicted. Emphasized: document-based investigation, direct policy impact.

**Lessons:**
- Judges prioritize demonstrable **impact** (policy change, parliamentary inquiry, arrests, public debate)
- **Original source material** (unpublished manuscripts, leaked documents, original data) scores highly
- **Cross-temporal/cross-border connections** show investigative ambition
- **Persistence despite obstruction** (withheld documents, denied access) matters for the "courage" criterion
